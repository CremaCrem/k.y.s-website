Usability Testing for K.Y.S

Explore the comprehensive usability testing process for K.Y.S Password Manager, including participant details, methodologies, tasks, results, and improvements.

Participant Background & Recruitment

We recruited 10 participants aged 18–45, with varying levels of technical expertise (3 beginners, 4 intermediate, 3 advanced). Participants included students, professionals, and casual users who regularly manage passwords.

  • Age Range: 18–45 years
  • Gender: 6 female, 4 male
  • Occupations: Students, IT professionals, office workers
  • Tech Familiarity: Beginner to advanced

Participants were recruited via university mailing lists, social media, and local tech meetups. Screening ensured a diverse group familiar with password management tools. Consent forms were signed, and participants were compensated with a $20 gift card.

DECIDE Framework

Overview

The DECIDE framework guided our usability testing to ensure a structured approach: Determine goals, Explore questions, Choose methods, Identify practical issues, Decide on ethics, and Evaluate data.

  • Determine: Improve user experience and interface efficiency.
  • Explore: How intuitive is the interface? Are tasks completed efficiently?
  • Choose: Task-based testing, observation, and post-test interviews.
  • Identify: Time constraints, participant availability, and testing environment.
  • Decide: Ensured informed consent and data anonymity.
  • Evaluate: Analyzed task completion rates, time, and user feedback.

Tasks & Scenarios

  • Add a new password for a social media account.
  • Search for an existing password in the "Bank" category.
  • Edit a password entry and update its category.
  • Delete a password and confirm the action.
  • Export the encrypted password file.

Participants were given realistic scenarios, such as: "You just created a new social media account and need to save its password securely," or "You suspect a bank account password is outdated and need to update it in the manager."

Usability Testing Results

Task Completion Rate

92% of tasks were completed successfully without assistance.

Average Task Time

Tasks took an average of 45 seconds, with search tasks being the fastest.

User Feedback

Users found the interface intuitive but suggested clearer delete confirmation prompts.

Prototype Improvements

  • Added a confirmation dialog for password deletion to prevent accidental loss.
  • Improved search functionality with real-time suggestions.
  • Enhanced category labels for better visibility on smaller screens.
  • Streamlined the export process with clearer instructions.
