Participant Background

We recruited 5 participants aged 19-28, consisting of undergraduate students and working professionals. All participants demonstrated good technical skills and familiarity with technology, making them well-suited for evaluating the password management application.

  • Age Range: 19-28 years
  • Gender: 2 female, 3 male
  • Occupations: Undergraduate students and working professionals
  • Tech Familiarity: Good technical skills and technology familiarity

DECIDE Framework

DECIDE Framework

D – Determine the Goals

The primary goal of the usability testing for the Keep Yourself Safe (K.Y.S.) Password Management Application is to evaluate the effectiveness of its core functionalities. Specifically, the testing aims to assess how easily and efficiently users can perform the following tasks:

  • Add a password
  • View a saved password
  • Delete an account password
  • Edit an account password
  • Copy an account password

The testing also seeks to identify any usability issues that may hinder user interaction with the system and gather qualitative feedback on user experience to inform future improvements.

E – Explore the Questions

The usability testing is designed to answer the following key questions:

  • How intuitive is the process of adding a password, including filling in website type, selecting a category, and entering username/email and password?
  • Can users easily locate and view saved passwords using the provided icon?
  • Is the process of deleting an account password straightforward, including finding the account and confirming deletion?
  • How effectively can users edit account password details, including locating the account and updating information?
  • Can users quickly copy account passwords using the copy icon?
  • What challenges or usability issues do users encounter while performing these tasks?
  • How do users perceive the overall ease of use and clarity of the K.Y.S. interface?
  • Does prior experience with password managers or comfort with technology influence task performance?

To gather background information, participants will be asked the following questions before testing:

  • Have you ever used a password manager before?
  • How do you currently keep track of your passwords?
  • What types of accounts do you typically have passwords for?
  • On a scale of 1-5, how would you rate your comfort with technology in general?

C – Choose the Evaluation Approach and Methods

The evaluation approach involves conducting in-person usability testing with a prototype of the K.Y.S. Password Management Application. The methods include:

  • Interviews and Task-Based Testing
  • Observation
  • Video and Audio Recording

I – Identify the Practical Issues

Participants
  • Number: 6 participants
  • Demographics: Undergraduate students from Partido State University
  • Recruitment: From local barangay due to logistical constraints
  • Requirements: Must be willing to use provided laptop with K.Y.S. prototype
Usability Testing Team
  • Observer: Monitors user behavior and identifies issues
  • Facilitator: Guides participants through testing process
  • Time Controller: Manages timing of tasks
  • Video Recorder: Captures video and photos

D – Decide How to Deal with the Ethical Issues

  • Informed Consent: Participants provided with consent form
  • Confidentiality: All data kept secure and confidential
  • Permission for Recording: Explicit permission required
  • Right to Withdraw: Participants can withdraw anytime
  • Clear Communication: Purpose and procedures explained
  • Minimal Risk: Low-risk activities only

Consent form signed by all participants

E – Evaluate, Analyze, Interpret, and Present the Data

  • Quantitative analysis of task completion times
  • Qualitative analysis of user feedback
  • Identification of usability issues
  • Recommendations for improvements

Tasks & Scenarios

  • Add Password:
    • Fill in Website type.
    • Select what category from the category drop-down.
    • Fill in Username/Email.
    • Fill in Password.
    • Click Add Password.
  • Delete Account Password:
    • Click the eye icon at the bottom.
    • Find the Account you would like to delete.
    • Click the trashcan icon at the upper right corner beside your selected account to delete.
    • Click confirm to delete.
  • Edit Account Password:
    • Click the eye icon at the bottom.
    • Search or find the Account you would like to edit.
    • Click the note and pen icon at the upper-right part of your selected account.
    • Fill in the parts you would like to edit.
    • Click Update Password.
  • Copy Account Password:
    • Click the eye icon at the bottom.
    • Search for the Account you would like to copy.
    • Click the two overlapping pieces of paper to copy.
    • Click Close.

Participants were given the following scenarios to test the K.Y.S Password Manager's functionality:

  • Add a streaming service password: "You just signed up for a new streaming service and need to add its login details securely to the password manager."
  • Remove an old email account password: "You want to remove an old email account's password from the manager because you no longer use it."
  • Update a bank account password: "You need to update the password for your bank account after resetting it online."
  • Copy a work account password: "You're logging into a work account and need to quickly copy its password to the clipboard."
